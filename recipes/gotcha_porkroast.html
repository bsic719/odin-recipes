Gotcha Pork!

Description

A creamy, seasoned mashed potato dish wrapped in an abundance of oily & fatty bacon finished with a glazed red wine sace!!!

Creative invention made by the main character Soma Yukihira. This is an interpretation and a specialized rendition of this epic masterpiece.

Follow the anime at: crunchyroll

Ingredients

    Gotcha Pork Roast:

    • 3 Medium Russet potatoes (peeled and cut in quarters)
    • Water for boiling
    • 1 tsp Salt
    • 1 pinch Pepper
    • 1 Garlic clove
    • 2 sprigs Fresh rosemary
    • 50 ml Whole milk
    • 2 tbsp Unsalted butter (1 tbsp each for potatoes & frying)
    • 1 tsp Chicken bouillon powder
    • 1/2 tsp Worcestershire sauce
    • 1/2 Onion finely diced
    • 1 Eryngii mushroom finely diced
    • 3 Garlic cloves finely diced
    • 1 tsp Parmesan cheese powdered or finely grated
    • 1 pinch Dry parsley
    • 6-8 Streaky bacon (long pieces preferred)

    Red Wine Sauce:

    • 150 ml Red wine
    • 1 tbsp Soy sauce
    • 1 tbsp Mirin
    • 1 tsp Sugar
    • 1 tbsp Unsalted butter

Steps

    Pork Roast

    1. Place the peeled/cut potatoes inside a large pot and fill with cold water unti lthe potatoes are fully submerged
    2. Add 1 tsp salt, a pinch of pepper, 1 sprig of rosemary and a whole garlic clove to the pot and place it on the stove
    3. Bring the water to a rolling boil and cook unti lthe potatoes are soft to the core. (This should take ~10-15 minutes depending on the size)
    4. Pour the potatoes into a colander to drain the water, give them a shake and discard the rosemary and garlic clove. Let the potatoes sit for 1-2 minutes to let out the excess steam.
    5. Transfer the potatoes to a large mixing bowl and mash until smooth.
    6. Add 50 ml of whole milk and 1 tbsp unssalted butter to a small microwavable bowl. Cover the bowl and microwave for 40 seconds at 500W or in intervals until the butter has melted.
    7. Add 1 tsp of chicken bouillon powder to the warm milk and butter to mix until dissolved.
    8. Pour the mixture into the potatoes and mix until smooth. Cover the bowel and set aside for later.
    9. Heat a frying pan on medium and add 1 tbsp unsalted butter and 1/2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce.
    10. Once the butter is melted, add diced onion and fry until slightly softened.
    11. Add 3 diced garlic cloves and 1 finely diced eryngii mushroom and fry everything together until the onions start to turn translucent.
    12. Remove the pan from the heat and allow to cool for a few minutes.
    13. Transfer the contents of the pan from the heat and allow to cool for a few minutes.
    14. Mix until all the ingredients are evenly distributed, cover the bowl so the mixture doesn't dry out and then leave to cool to room temperature.
    15. Take a sheet of plastic wrap and place the mixture in the middle. Shape into a thick cylnider and firmly wrap.
    16. Chill in the fridge for about 30 minutes.

    Baking

    1. Preheat your oven to 180 deg Celsius (Approx 355 F).
    2. Remove your mashed potato from the fridge and wrap each end with 1-2 rashers of bacon. Wrap the rest of the bacon (4 long rashers) around the cylinder of mashed potato and try to make sure there aren't any gaps.
    3. Wrap the bacon with butchers twine to keep it secure and place 1 sprig of rosemary on the top
    4. Transfer the gotcha pork roast to a roasting tin and plce it in the oven on the top shelf. Bake for 20 minutes or until the bacron is golden

    Red Wine Sauce

    1. Heat a small saucepan on medium and add 1 tbsp butter.
    2. Once the butter is melted, add 150ml red wine, 1 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tbsp mirin, and 1 tsp sugar
    3. Bring to a boil and allow it to bubble for a few minutes to burn off the alcohol in the wine and mirin.
    4. Remove from the heat to cool aside.

    Serving

    1. Take the gotcha pork roast from the oven and allow to rest for a few minutes.
    2. Cut off the butchers twine and transfer it to a serving plate. (Use a spatula to help you lift it.)
    3. Drizzle with the red wine sauce.
    4. ENJOY!
